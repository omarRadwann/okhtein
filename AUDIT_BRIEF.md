# Cold Audit — FitSole Vault (be brutally honest)

> Paste this whole file into a brand-new Claude session that has the browser tool. Do not give it any other context.

You are doing a **first-impressions expert review** of a live website you have never seen. You know nothing about how it was built or why any decision was made — that's the point. Approach it as a demanding **senior creative director + 3D web-experience critic** who has judged hundreds of award-winning sites. Your job is to find what's weak, broken, confusing, or unconvincing, and say it plainly. Flattery is useless to the client; the hard truth is what makes work world-class.

## What you're reviewing

**FitSole Cairo** — a scroll-driven 3D "sneaker vault": as you scroll, a camera travels through a 3D space, and it's also meant to be a real shoppable store. Positioning: a premium, high-end sneaker destination. That's all you're told — judge for yourself whether it delivers on "premium" and whether it works as a store.

**Live URL:** https://omarradwann.github.io/fitsole-vault/

## How to test (use the browser tool)

1. Open the URL. Wait for it to fully load — 3D assets take a few seconds. Note the load experience: blank screen? spinner? how long until something appears?
2. **Scroll slowly from top to bottom**, pausing at each distinct "scene." Screenshot each one. Then **scroll back up** — does reverse work cleanly, or break?
3. **Resize the window narrow** (phone / tablet width) and repeat the scroll — does the 3D + the text hold up, or fall apart?
4. **Interact with everything**: every nav item, the search, brand logos, the shop / product browsing, and try to add an item to cart / reach checkout. Note anything dead, broken, or confusing.
5. Capture screenshot evidence for every issue you raise.

## Judge these five areas — be specific, cite screenshots

1. **Design (visual craft).** Typography (hierarchy, pairing, legibility over a moving 3D background), color + contrast, spacing / layout, composition and framing of each scene, polish + consistency. Does it look genuinely premium, or generic / amateur? Where does the eye go — and is that right?
2. **Motion.** Scroll feel (smooth vs janky / stuttering), camera movement (elegant vs disorienting / nauseating), easing + pacing, transitions between scenes, how much you scroll vs how much happens (intuitive?), parallax. Does the motion elevate the experience or get in the way?
3. **Graphics / 3D.** Model quality (the sneakers — detail, materials, realism), lighting believability, reflections / surfaces, render crispness vs blur / aliasing, richness vs emptiness of the space, any artifacts / clipping / flicker. Does it read as high-end CGI or a hobby-grade 3D demo?
4. **Storytelling / narrative.** Is there a clear arc and a payoff? Do the scenes connect to one another? Does the copy advance a story and land emotionally? Is it obvious what FitSole is and why you should care? Would a first-time visitor be intrigued — or confused and lost? Does the experience lead you toward shopping?
5. **Experience & commerce (secondary — but flag everything).** Can you actually shop? Are the CTAs obvious and working? Navigation clarity, dead ends, responsive / mobile behavior, accessibility (text contrast, motion sensitivity, can you use it at all without the 3D?). Bottom line: does this site turn a visitor into a buyer?

## Rules of engagement

- **Be ruthlessly honest.** Do not pad with praise. If something looks cheap, say so and explain *why*.
- For each issue, give **(a) what you observed** (with a screenshot), **(b) why it's a problem** (impact on the user or the brand), and **(c) a concrete fix.**
- **Rate severity:** 🔴 Critical (breaks the experience / would lose the visitor) · 🟠 Major (clearly hurts quality) · 🟡 Minor (polish).
- Note briefly what genuinely works — but spend your energy on the problems.
- **Benchmark against the best:** scroll-driven 3D brand / commerce sites that win Awwwards "Site of the Day." Would *this* win? If not, name the specific gaps.

## Deliver

1. **Verdict** (2–3 sentences): your gut first impression + the single biggest problem.
2. **Issues by area** (the five above), severity-rated, with screenshots + fixes.
3. **Fix-first list:** the top 5 things to change, in priority order.
4. **Score:** rate each of the five areas out of 10, each with a one-line justification.

Don't hold back.
